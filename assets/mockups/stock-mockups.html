Roasted beet salad with whipped feta

NYT modified · 3 changes · serves 4 · cooked 6×
Cook
⚖ Bench
Scale
Ingredients
680g
red beets, scrubbed
200g
feta · upped from 150g (Apr)
60g
greek yogurt
3 tbsp
olive oil · good stuff, not blend (Mar)
1
lemon, zest + juice
2 tsp
honey · added; original had none (Apr)
¼ cup
pistachios, toasted
—
flaky salt, pepper, dill
Method
1
Roast beets at 400°F, wrapped in foil, until a knife slides in clean — 60–80 min depending on size.
2
Whip feta with yogurt, lemon zest, half the oil. Should be spoonable, not stiff.
3
Peel beets while warm (skins slip off). Cut into wedges, toss with lemon juice, honey, remaining oil.
My notes
The honey is the unlock — original is too sharp. Plate the feta first, beets on top, juices pool nicely. Pistachios at the very end or they go soft.

Options · ranked by closeness
1Milk + lemon juice
240g milk + 15g lemon juice (or white vinegar). Stand 5 min until curdled. Closest in acidity and water content — texture nearly identical for whipped applications.
2Plain yogurt, thinned
180g full-fat yogurt + 60g water or milk. Slightly tangier, slightly denser. Makes the feta richer — actually good here.
3Kefir
240g, 1:1 sub. Thinner than buttermilk, more probiotic tang. Stir, don't whip too hard or it breaks.
